Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

Designing required me to become familiar with adobe and the creative cloud. Technology became my main medium for designing. Additionally, Creating designs for others requires me to ensure that my designs usuability are accessible and respectful. In my cartoon portrait I had to make the background a solid blue because it was taken in a coffee shop and there were people in the back, additionally the UX testing project for my participant description I didnt add any photos or names and kept the descriptions very general.Whether that was changing the background fo a design because people were in it, in my design fuego I added alternative text to be more accessible, and in my good design project making sure theres a big enough contrast between tones to ensure readability.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

Learning the principles of design I was able to experiment with it within my work. After reading about common things new designers often do I tried to avoid it and try new things. I tried to not always use center alignment and experimented with different alignments. I also tried using different fonts and tried playing with color schemes and using complimentary colors. For example in the good design project I tried doing left alignment and incorporated complementary colors.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

By engaging with other during workshops I was able gain insight/feedback from my classmates to expand on my designs. While I thought something looked fine or was good how it is, when speaking with others there were things that were pointed out that I didnt notice/ advice to improve was given. I can to workshop with a protoype design and left with new ideas for a more refined piece of work. With the good design project, it was still a work in progress when I shared it during workshop. My classmates gave me advice like putting an outline around the frame, making the boxes and sub title the same color, and moving the frames around which I incorporated into my final design.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Through the revision process, this was a chance for me to take my original design and enhance it. With my good design project it required me to use a different adobe software to edit my design to make my own PNJs to add to my design spread.

5. Learn to present your work.

Through these design projects, workshops, and discussions I slowly figured out how to present my works to others. I presented my design to others in a way that truly expresses my work and presented it in a way I want my not only my designs but myself to be seen as a designer. For example, with all of my designs so far sharing them in workshop I had to explain my designs, thought process, and explain why I did things the way I did because everyone thinks differently.
